Greek God Scramblee

Document

Eggs are bland, but this medditeranean-influenced scramble works for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Prepared in 10 minutes, serves 2. Serve with cruty olive bread or toast

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups packed baby spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced (about 1/2 cup)
  • 1/4 cup reduced ft or regular Crumbled Feta Cheese
  • 1 tsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 tsp olive oil
  • Pinch salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Whisk eggs, salt, and pepper in a small mixing bowl. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes until spinach is wilted
  3. Add tomatoes and eggs. Reduce heat to medium low and cook to desired doneness, stirring gently
  4. Sprinkle with feta cheee and parsley

Nutrition Snapshot

Per serving: 206 calories, 14g fat, 4g saturated fat, 17g protein, 6g carbs, 3g fiber, 3g sufr, 622 mg sodium.
